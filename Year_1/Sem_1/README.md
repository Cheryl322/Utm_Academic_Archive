## 🧠 Semester Reflection & Gained Knowledge

### 1. Key Insights Gained
This semester successfully transformed my perspective on computing from a casual user into an analytical developer. By building foundational **Problem-Solving Skills** through the Design Thinking framework and basic pseudo-codes, I learned that software engineering is entirely about dissecting complex problems systematically before deploying code[cite: 3]. Moving from structural gate circuits in Digital Logic to functional procedural arrays in C++ provided me with a robust technical baseline regarding how low-level systems handle data streams and memory[cite: 3].

### 2. Actionable Suggestions for Improvement
Reflecting on this academic block, I realized that my technical output could be further optimized by introducing automated version control earlier. While learning fundamental syntax control rules is crucial, practicing concurrent pipeline storage (like pushing small lab scripts via Git/GitHub) right from Semester 1 would significantly accelerate adaptation to enterprise environments.

### 3. Industry Alignment
Through the Industry Engagements under the TIS module (including compiling formal technical papers in IEEE format and site visit analyses), I gained valuable macro-insights into real-world agile engineering setups and scalable cloud data pipelines[cite: 2]. This has heavily inspired me to pursue short-term specialized certifications on professional platforms during my personal breaks to stay tightly aligned with evolving corporate talent benchmarks.
